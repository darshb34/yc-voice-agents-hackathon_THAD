# Healthy Meal Recipes

A collection of 12 balanced, whole-food recipes with full nutrition metadata.
Each recipe lists per-serving macros, diet classification, prep/cook times, and allergens.

> **Note:** Nutrition values are estimates based on standard ingredient quantities and
> typical brands. Actual values vary with portion size, substitutions, and exact products.
> Macros are rounded; calories ≈ (protein × 4) + (carbs × 4) + (fat × 9).

## 1. Lemon-Herb Baked Salmon with Quinoa & Asparagus

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Diet | Pescatarian, GF, DF |
| Vegan / Vegetarian / Pescatarian | N / N / Y |
| Servings | 2 |
| Prep / Cook / Total | 10 min / 20 min / 30 min |
| Calories per serving | 520 |
| Protein (g) | 42 |
| Fat (g) | 24 |
| Carbs (g) | 32 |
| Fiber (g) | 6 |
| Allergens | Fish |
| Why it's healthy | Omega-3 rich salmon, complete-protein quinoa, fiber from asparagus |

**Ingredients**
- 2 salmon fillets (6 oz each)
- 1/2 cup dry quinoa, rinsed
- 1 bunch asparagus, trimmed
- 1 lemon (juice + zest)
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp fresh dill, chopped
- Salt & pepper to taste

**Instructions**
1. Preheat oven to 400°F (205°C). Cook quinoa in 1 cup water, ~15 min, then fluff.
2. Place salmon and asparagus on a lined sheet pan. Drizzle with olive oil, garlic, lemon juice/zest, salt, and pepper.
3. Bake 12–15 min until salmon flakes easily and asparagus is tender-crisp.
4. Top salmon with fresh dill and serve over quinoa.

## 2. Chickpea & Quinoa Power Bowl with Tahini Dressing

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Diet | Vegan, GF, DF |
| Vegan / Vegetarian / Pescatarian | Y / Y / Y |
| Servings | 2 |
| Prep / Cook / Total | 15 min / 15 min / 30 min |
| Calories per serving | 480 |
| Protein (g) | 19 |
| Fat (g) | 20 |
| Carbs (g) | 58 |
| Fiber (g) | 14 |
| Allergens | Sesame (tahini) |
| Why it's healthy | Plant protein + fiber from chickpeas and quinoa, healthy fats from tahini |

**Ingredients**
- 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
- 1/2 cup dry quinoa, rinsed
- 2 cups baby spinach
- 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
- 1/2 cucumber, diced
- 1/4 red onion, thinly sliced
- Dressing: 2 tbsp tahini, 1 tbsp lemon juice, 1 clove garlic, 2–3 tbsp water, salt

**Instructions**
1. Cook quinoa in 1 cup water, ~15 min; let cool slightly.
2. Optional: roast chickpeas at 425°F for 20 min with a little olive oil and paprika for crunch.
3. Whisk tahini, lemon, garlic, water, and salt into a pourable dressing.
4. Assemble bowls with quinoa, spinach, tomatoes, cucumber, onion, and chickpeas. Drizzle with dressing.

## 3. Tofu & Broccoli Stir-Fry with Brown Rice

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Diet | Vegan, DF (GF with tamari) |
| Vegan / Vegetarian / Pescatarian | Y / Y / Y |
| Servings | 3 |
| Prep / Cook / Total | 15 min / 15 min / 30 min |
| Calories per serving | 430 |
| Protein (g) | 22 |
| Fat (g) | 16 |
| Carbs (g) | 52 |
| Fiber (g) | 8 |
| Allergens | Soy, Sesame, Wheat (use tamari to make GF) |
| Why it's healthy | High plant protein, whole-grain rice, minimal added fat |

**Ingredients**
- 14 oz extra-firm tofu, cubed and pressed
- 4 cups broccoli florets
- 1 red bell pepper, sliced
- 1 cup dry brown rice
- 3 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce or tamari
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp grated ginger
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp cornstarch + 2 tbsp water

**Instructions**
1. Cook brown rice per package (~40 min, or use a rice cooker).
2. Pan-sear tofu in a little oil until golden on all sides; set aside.
3. Stir-fry broccoli and pepper with ginger and garlic 4–5 min.
4. Add soy sauce, sesame oil, and cornstarch slurry; return tofu and toss until glossy. Serve over rice.

## 4. Red Lentil & Coconut Curry

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Diet | Vegan, GF, DF |
| Vegan / Vegetarian / Pescatarian | Y / Y / Y |
| Servings | 4 |
| Prep / Cook / Total | 10 min / 25 min / 35 min |
| Calories per serving | 390 |
| Protein (g) | 16 |
| Fat (g) | 14 |
| Carbs (g) | 50 |
| Fiber (g) | 12 |
| Allergens | Tree nut (coconut) |
| Why it's healthy | Lentils provide protein + iron + fiber; turmeric and ginger are anti-inflammatory |

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 cups dry red lentils, rinsed
- 1 can (14 oz) light coconut milk
- 1 onion, diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp grated ginger
- 2 tbsp curry powder, 1 tsp turmeric
- 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
- 3 cups baby spinach
- 2 cups vegetable broth

**Instructions**
1. Sauté onion, garlic, and ginger until soft. Stir in curry powder and turmeric for 1 min.
2. Add lentils, tomatoes, coconut milk, and broth. Simmer 18–20 min until lentils break down.
3. Stir in spinach until wilted. Season with salt; serve over rice or with naan.

| Servings | 2 |

| Fat (g) | 14 |
| Carbs (g) | 12 |
| Fiber (g) | 3 |
| Allergens | Fish |

**Ingredients**
- 2 cod fillets (6 oz each)
- 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
- 1/4 cup Kalamata olives, pitted

**Instructions**

4. Bake 18–20 min until cod is opaque and flakes. Garnish with parsley.

## 7. Grilled Chicken & Vegetables with Roasted Sweet Potato

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Diet | Omnivore, GF, DF |
| Vegan / Vegetarian / Pescatarian | N / N / N |
| Servings | 2 |
| Prep / Cook / Total | 15 min / 25 min / 40 min |
| Calories per serving | 480 |
| Protein (g) | 45 |
| Fat (g) | 14 |
| Carbs (g) | 42 |
| Fiber (g) | 7 |
| Allergens | None |
| Why it's healthy | Lean protein, complex carbs from sweet potato, no refined ingredients |

**Ingredients**
- 2 boneless skinless chicken breasts (6 oz each)
- 2 medium sweet potatoes, cubed
- 1 zucchini, sliced
- 1 red bell pepper, sliced
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1 tsp garlic powder
- Salt & pepper

**Instructions**
1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Toss sweet potatoes with 1 tbsp oil and roast 25 min.
2. Season chicken with paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper; grill or pan-sear ~6 min per side to 165°F internal.
3. Sauté zucchini and pepper in remaining oil until tender-crisp.
4. Slice chicken and plate with vegetables and sweet potato.

## 8. Ahi Tuna Poke Bowl

| Vegan / Vegetarian / Pescatarian | N / N / Y |
| Servings | 2 |
| Prep / Cook / Total | 20 min / 15 min (rice) / 35 min |
| Calories per serving | 510 |
| Protein (g) | 34 |
| Fat (g) | 16 |
| Carbs (g) | 55 |
| Fiber (g) | 6 |
| Allergens | Fish, Soy, Sesame |
| Why it's healthy | Lean raw tuna, healthy fats from avocado, balanced macros |

**Ingredients**
- 10 oz sushi-grade ahi tuna, cubed
- 1 cup dry sushi or brown rice
- 1 avocado, sliced
- 1/2 cup shelled edamame
- 1/2 cucumber, diced
- 1/4 cup shredded carrot
- Marinade: 2 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tsp sesame oil, 1 tsp rice vinegar, scallions, sesame seeds

**Instructions**

4. Garnish with extra scallions and sesame seeds.

## 9. Black Bean & Sweet Potato Tacos

|-------|-------|

| Vegan / Vegetarian / Pescatarian | Y / Y / Y |
| Servings | 3 (2 tacos each) |
| Prep / Cook / Total | 15 min / 25 min / 40 min |
| Calories per serving | 410 |
| Protein (g) | 14 |
| Fat (g) | 12 |
| Carbs (g) | 64 |
| Fiber (g) | 14 |
| Allergens | Corn (tortillas) |
| Why it's healthy | High fiber and plant protein, beta-carotene from sweet potato, no animal fat |

**Ingredients**
- 2 medium sweet potatoes, diced
- 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
- 6 small corn tortillas
- 1 tbsp olive oil

## 10. Turkey & Quinoa Stuffed Bell Peppers

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Diet | Omnivore, GF (DF without cheese) |
| Vegan / Vegetarian / Pescatarian | N / N / N |
| Servings | 4 |
| Prep / Cook / Total | 15 min / 35 min / 50 min |
| Calories per serving | 380 |
| Protein (g) | 32 |
| Fat (g) | 14 |
| Carbs (g) | 32 |
| Fiber (g) | 6 |
| Allergens | Dairy (optional cheese topping) |
| Why it's healthy | Lean ground turkey, fiber-rich quinoa and peppers, portion-controlled |

**Ingredients**
- 4 large bell peppers, halved and seeded
- 1 lb lean (93%) ground turkey
- 3/4 cup dry quinoa, rinsed
- 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
- 1 onion, diced; 2 cloves garlic
- 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp oregano
- Optional: 1/2 cup shredded mozzarella

**Instructions**
1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Cook quinoa in 1.5 cups water, ~15 min.
2. Brown turkey with onion and garlic; add tomatoes, cumin, oregano, and cooked quinoa.
3. Stuff pepper halves with the mixture; top with cheese if using.
4. Bake 25–30 min until peppers are tender.

## 11. Shrimp & Zucchini Noodle Bowl with Pesto

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Diet | Pescatarian, GF (low-carb) |
| Vegan / Vegetarian / Pescatarian | N / N / Y |
| Servings | 2 |
| Prep / Cook / Total | 15 min / 10 min / 25 min |
| Calories per serving | 360 |
| Protein (g) | 30 |
| Fat (g) | 22 |
| Carbs (g) | 14 |
| Fiber (g) | 4 |
| Allergens | Shellfish, Tree nut (pine nuts), Dairy (parmesan in pesto) |
| Why it's healthy | High protein, very low carb, vegetable-based "noodles" |

**Ingredients**
- 12 oz raw shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 3 medium zucchini, spiralized
- 1/4 cup basil pesto
- 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 clove garlic
- Lemon and black pepper

**Instructions**
1. Sauté shrimp with garlic in olive oil 2–3 min per side until pink; remove.
2. Add zucchini noodles to the pan and cook 2 min until just tender (don't overcook).
3. Toss noodles with pesto and tomatoes; return shrimp.
4. Finish with lemon and black pepper.

## 12. Spinach, Mushroom & Feta Egg-White Frittata

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Diet | Vegetarian, GF |
| Vegan / Vegetarian / Pescatarian | N / Y / Y |
| Servings | 4 |
| Prep / Cook / Total | 10 min / 20 min / 30 min |
| Calories per serving | 220 |
| Protein (g) | 20 |
| Fat (g) | 12 |
| Carbs (g) | 8 |
| Fiber (g) | 2 |
| Allergens | Egg, Dairy |
| Why it's healthy | High protein, low carb, loaded with vegetables; great make-ahead meal |

**Ingredients**
- 8 egg whites + 2 whole eggs
- 2 cups baby spinach
- 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
- 1/3 cup crumbled feta
- 1/4 onion, diced
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- Salt & pepper

**Instructions**
1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). In an oven-safe skillet, sauté onion and mushrooms until soft.
2. Add spinach until wilted.
3. Whisk eggs and egg whites with salt and pepper; pour over vegetables. Sprinkle feta on top.
4. Cook 2 min on the stovetop, then transfer to the oven 15 min until set. Slice into 4 wedges.
